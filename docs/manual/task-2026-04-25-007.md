## Context

`docs/perf/mobile-matrix-2026-04-25.md` documents the emulated mobile
sweep (Playwright × WebKit/Chromium across 5 viewports). The matrix
explicitly calls out four classes of behavior that emulation can't
verify:

1. **iOS Safari URL-bar collapse on first scroll.** The matrix uses a
   132 px chrome budget for iPhone SE (worst-case URL bar visible +
   bottom toolbar). After a downward swipe, real Safari collapses the
   URL bar to ~52 px, recovering ~80 px. This determines whether the
   soft-fail "hero CTA bottom = 562 px vs 535 px usable" is a real
   problem or a transient pre-scroll state.
2. **Soft-keyboard layout shift on form fields.** Contact form + checkout
   form inputs trigger iOS keyboard. Emulation doesn't reproduce the
   viewport resize.
3. **Rubber-band overscroll.** Elastic bounce on the catalog scroll +
   overlay scroll containers; behavior depends on the surface having
   `overflow-y: auto` vs `scroll`.
4. **Touch-and-hold + force-touch gestures.** Long-press menus on
   product images, link previews, etc.

This task is the falsifying signal for those four classes.

## What you're doing

Walk through the site's mobile experience on a real iPhone running
real Safari. Confirm or refute the matrix's emulated findings. Capture
screenshots where the real-device behavior differs from the captured
matrix screenshots.

## Devices to use

In priority order:

1. **iPhone SE (1st-gen or SE 2 — both have 375 × 667 viewport).** The
   load-bearing device for the fold-line story.
2. **A current-gen iPhone (any 13/14/15/16 series).** Sanity check that
   newer phones aren't worse; matrix says they're fine in emulation.
3. **An Android phone (any modern Pixel/Samsung).** Sanity check on
   Chrome mobile vs Playwright's Chromium emulation.

If you only have one device, use #1.

## Steps

### 1. Open the site at https://iampatterson.com on iPhone SE Safari

Brand-new tab. Don't pre-clear the URL bar. Capture the **first paint
state** (no scroll yet).

> Copy assertions below were captured against the homepage state on
> 2026-04-25. If hero copy has drifted in a later phase, treat the
> shape of the test (does the H1 + kicker + body + both CTAs render
> above the chrome-aware fold) as load-bearing — not the literal
> strings. Whatever the current homepage says, all of it should fit.

**Confirm or refute:**

- Hero H1 ("I build / measurement / infrastructure.") fully visible
- Italic kicker ("This site runs on the same stack I sell...") visible
- Body paragraph ("Instead of describing it, I built on it...")
  visible
- "See your session →" primary CTA visible
- "Explore the demo" ghost CTA visible

Matrix prediction: H1 + kicker + body visible at first paint; "See
your session" CTA partially or fully cut off by Safari's bottom
toolbar; "Explore the demo" below the fold.

### 2. Tiny scroll down (~50 px swipe)

Goal: trigger Safari's URL-bar collapse without scrolling past the hero.

**Confirm or refute:**

- URL bar visibly collapses
- Both CTAs come into view
- No layout reflow / no jumpy hero typography

Matrix prediction: URL-bar collapses at ~52 px chrome from ~88 px
chrome, gaining ~36 px of usable height; both CTAs reachable post-
collapse.

### 3. Tap "See your session →"

**Confirm or refute:**

- Overlay opens (CRT boot animation plays cleanly, ~260 ms)
- Overview tab is the default-active tab
- Tab row fits cleanly: `OVERVIEW · TIMELINE · CONSENT` all on one
  line, no row-overflow scroll

### 4. Switch to Consent tab

**Confirm or refute:**

- Per-tag consent rows render
- Sent / Blocked destination chips render
- "Browser storage" inspector renders below the destination chips
- App-identity group has at least 2-3 keys (`_iap_aid`, `_iap_sid`,
  `iampatterson.session_state`)
- Long values truncate cleanly with a "reveal" button
- Tapping a "reveal" button expands the value
- No row spills sideways outside its container

This is the D9 + D7 surface check. The matrix's regression pin caught
the take-1 + take-2 spill bugs; this verifies they don't reappear under
real touch input.

### 5. Close the overlay, navigate to /demo/ecommerce

**Confirm or refute:**

- Demo banner ("← back to homepage · this is a demo · nothing ships
  from here") wraps cleanly on mobile (back-link on top, message
  below, per D8.g)
- Catalog grid renders 1-column at 375 px wide
- "+ add to cart" buttons are tappable (≥ 44 × 44 actual)

### 6. Tap a product, add to cart, navigate to /demo/ecommerce/cart

**Confirm or refute:**

- Cart line items stack cleanly (image+name on row 1, qty+price on
  row 2 per the cart-view's mobile shape)
- "proceed to checkout →" CTA is tappable
- Live sidebar ("data quality · 6 assertions running") renders below
  the cart on mobile (not beside)

### 7. Tap a form field on /contact

**Confirm or refute:**

- iOS keyboard slides up
- Form field stays visible (browser scrolls to keep the focused field
  in view)
- The "Send" button is reachable without keyboard dismissal

### 8. (Optional, if time permits) Long-press on a product image

**Confirm or refute:**

- iOS's "Add to Photos" / "Save Image" / "Share" context menu appears
  cleanly
- No accidental drag-start or layout shift
